About the challenge

The VAST Challenge results in 2017 revealed that the Kasios Furniture manufacturing firm may have been a major cause to the apparent decline in the number of Rose-Crested Blue Pipit nesting pairs, a beloved bird of Mistford residents and visitors to the Boonsong Lekagul Nature Preserve. Kasios allegedly employed Methylosmolene, a prohibited chemical, in their production process.They smuggled process waste into the Preserve's northeast corner, and methylosmolene was found in their chimney emissions.

Continued....

Kasios now alleges that the study was incorrect and skewed in his favor. Kasios has undertaken its own "study" into the Pipit issue in response to these conclusions, and they now say that there are lots of Rose-crested Blue Pipits contentedly living and nesting in the Preserve. They have given a collection of Pipit bird cries that were recently captured around the Preserve, along with the places where they were recorded, to back up their claim. They assert that the Pipits are clearly a thriving population, and Kasios will offer additional proof as their inquiry continues. In last year's Challenge, Mitch Vogel, an ornithology graduate student from Mistford College, found the Pipit's condition and conducted an inquiry. Normally, we'd enlist Mitch's assistance to verify Kasios' assertion. Unfortunately, Mitch is working in a distant region without internet connectivity for a lengthy period of time and cannot be reached easily.

The Pangera Ornithology Conservation Society, which funded Mitch last year, is at a loss for what to do in light of this development. The townfolk and Preserve rangers appear to agree that Kasios' statements are supported by the recordings. Mistford College is unable to get the assistance of another Pipit specialist. They do, however, have a collection of bird cries from the Preserve that have been reviewed as having correct identifications by several ornithology organisations. They've heard that new machine learning and visual analytics approaches can be used in circumstances like these. These technologies might perhaps be used to classify and analyze the calls, which could then be evaluated when Mitch returns.

Tasks and Questions:

  1. Using the bird call collection and the included map of the Wildlife Preserve, characterize the patterns of all of the bird species in the Preserve over the time of the collection. Please assume we have a reasonable distribution of sensors and human collectors providing the recordings, so that the patterns are reasonably representative of the bird locations across the area. Do you detect any trends or anomalies in the patterns?
  2. Turn your attention to the set of bird calls supplied by Kasios. Does this set support the claim of Pipits being found across the Preserve? A machine learning approach using the bird call library may help your investigation. What is the role of visualization in your analysis of the Kasios bird calls?
  3. Formulate a hypotheses concerning the state of the Rose Crested Blue Pipit. What are your primary pieces of evidence to support your assertion? What next steps should be taken in the investigation to either support or refute the Kasios claim that the Pipits are actually thriving across the Boonsong Lekagul Wildlife Preserve?
